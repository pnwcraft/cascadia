Seattle fence permit and neighbor planning guide.

Updated May 11, 2026 • Fence planning

A smooth fence project starts before demolition. Confirm the approximate fence line, talk with neighbors when a shared fence is involved, and decide where gates, latches, and privacy screens should go.

Estimate prep checklist

  • Measure approximate linear feet.
  • Count gates and note swing direction.
  • Collect style photos for vertical, horizontal, or good-neighbor fencing.
  • Note slope, retaining walls, trees, utilities, and access constraints.
  • Decide whether you want repair, partial replacement, or full replacement.

Neighbors and shared fences

Shared fence conversations are easier when you bring clear options: height, style, which side faces each yard, schedule, and how demolition debris will be handled. A good-neighbor fence can look finished on both sides.

Permits and rules

Rules can vary by height, location, front yard, corner visibility, easements, and HOA requirements. Verify current requirements before construction, especially if the fence is unusually tall or close to a public right-of-way.
