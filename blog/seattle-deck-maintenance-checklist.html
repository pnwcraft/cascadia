Seattle deck maintenance checklist.

Updated May 11, 2026 • Maintenance

Seattle decks last longer when water can leave quickly and small issues are handled before they become structural repairs. A simple seasonal routine helps protect boards, stairs, fasteners, railings, and the area where the deck meets the house.

Spring

  • Clear leaves from gaps, stairs, and against the house.
  • Check for soft boards, popped fasteners, and slippery algae.
  • Inspect rail posts, stair stringers, and ledger flashing.
  • Look under the deck for standing water, blocked ventilation, or soil contact.

Summer

  • Wash the walking surface with the material manufacturer’s recommended cleaner.
  • Schedule staining or sealing for cedar when the forecast is dry enough.
  • Check that furniture feet and grills are not trapping moisture against boards.

Fall

  • Trim vegetation away from boards and posts.
  • Confirm downspouts do not drain onto the deck.
  • Schedule repairs before winter storms.
