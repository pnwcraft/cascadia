Composite vs. cedar decking in Seattle.

Updated May 11, 2026 • Materials guide

Seattle deck materials need to handle rain, shade, moss pressure, freeze-thaw swings, and heavy daily use. Cedar and composite can both be excellent choices when the frame, ventilation, fasteners, flashing, and drainage details are planned correctly.

Choose cedar if you want a natural Northwest look

Cedar is warm, repairable, and visually classic. It can be a strong fit for homeowners who like natural color, traditional deck boards, and the ability to repair individual areas. The tradeoff is maintenance: cleaning, staining, and checking high-moisture areas matter in shaded Seattle yards.

Choose composite if low maintenance is the priority

Composite decking costs more up front but can reduce staining and board replacement over time. It pairs well with modern railings, hidden fastener systems, picture-frame borders, and homeowners who want a consistent finish.

Best answer: compare total lifecycle cost

Ask for both options in your estimate, including framing repairs, railing, stairs, disposal, and maintenance expectations. The best material is the one that fits the home, budget, sun exposure, and maintenance style.
